# Image Wishlist — Levent Marine (Portrait / 9:16)

> **Purpose.** Generate these images in Gemini Imagen 3 or ChatGPT
> (DALL-E 3 portrait), download with the **exact filename** in the
> "Save as" column, then drop the files into the **exact path** shown.
> The site picks them up automatically — no code change needed.
>
> **NEW dimensions — please read.** The right-side image deck on
> `/services` and `/service-wizard` is now a narrow vertical column.
> All photos must be **portrait, aspect ratio 9:16**, ideally
> **1080 × 1920** (or 1024 × 1792 if your generator only offers DALL-E
> sizes). JPG, q85, sRGB.
>
> If you've already generated landscape (4:3) versions — they will
> still work because of `object-cover`, but vertical framing reads
> dramatically better in the narrow column. Prefer portrait.
>
> **Style guide for every prompt** (applies to all entries):
> - Photorealistic, cinematic, documentary feel, **portrait composition**.
> - Subject framed **vertically** — leave headroom and foreground
>   space, the lens is high or low to give a tall image natural shape.
> - Navy + amber palette in the scene (cool maritime steel, ambient
>   warm work-light or amber control panels).
> - No watermarks, no dummy logos, no captions in the photo.
> - No people facing the camera (we want them at work, hands only).

## 15 · Practical tips for the generator

**Gemini Imagen 3** (web app):
- Aspect ratio menu: pick **9:16**.
- Resolution will be 1024×1792 — fine for the site, will sharpen.
- Generate 4 variations, pick the best.

**ChatGPT DALL-E 3**:
- Ask explicitly: "Generate this image in PORTRAIT 9:16 aspect ratio,
  1024×1792 pixels."
- DALL-E will sometimes ignore size unless you specify in the prompt.

**Avoid:**
- Square (1:1) — gets cropped badly in the narrow column.
- Landscape (16:9 / 4:3) — works but looks empty in the column.
- Faces / branding / readable text in the image.

## 16 · Drop-in instructions (unchanged)

1. Open Gemini Imagen 3 or ChatGPT DALL-E 3.
2. Paste the prompt **including the format paragraph from §2**.
3. Generate 4 variations; pick the best.
4. Download as JPG, rename to the **Save as** filename.
5. Drop the file into the **Place at** path.
6. Commit + push. Site picks it up next deploy.

The deck gracefully skips missing files. New systems (smoke detection,
water mist) won't appear in the deck rotation until their image
arrives — once you drop the file, I wire it into the SERVICE_IMAGE
map and it joins the carousel automatically.
